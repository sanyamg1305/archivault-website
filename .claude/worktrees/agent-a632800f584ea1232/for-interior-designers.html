Where projects actually break

Where interior design projects actually break down

01

"Material decisions happen in WhatsApp and get disputed at installation"

The client approved the tile over a voice note. Three weeks later, the wrong tile is installed. The WhatsApp thread is 400 messages deep and proves nothing conclusive. You absorb the cost or the relationship - sometimes both.

02

"Clients change their mind and you have no record of what was agreed"

Scope creep is almost always undocumented change. Without a written approval trail, every "but I thought we decided..." conversation costs you time, money, or both. And it happens on nearly every project without a system.

03

"Vendor coordination is chaos across 8 different WhatsApp groups"

There is no single source of truth. Vendor A has the old spec. Vendor B has the revised spec. Nobody is sure which is final. So things get made to the wrong version and you deal with it at site.

04

"Budget conversations are the most dreaded part of every project"

Clients feel ambushed when they see numbers at the end of a long project. That feeling - even when the design is perfect - is what stops them from referring you to someone they care about.

The fix

What Archivault gives interior design studios

A structured system that replaces the WhatsApp threads, the scattered spreadsheets, and the approval screenshots that never quite prove anything.

Material tracking per room

Every material has a name, brand, vendor, cost, reference image, and approval status. Organised by room. Always current. No spreadsheet, no folder of screenshots - one structured view.

Timestamped approval workflow

Client approves with one click or requests revision with a written note. Every action is timestamped with user identity. No more "final kya decide hua tha?" - the answer is always one click away.

Print-ready material schedule

Export the full material schedule as a PDF organised by room. Hand it directly to your contractor. No transcription errors, no "which version is this?" - it comes straight out of the approved record.

Budget dashboard with room-level breakdown

Total budget, approved spend, and remaining balance visible at all times. Colour-coded alerts at 75% and 90% spend per room. Client sees this too - so budget conversations happen throughout the project, not at the end.

Design version control

Upload renders and drawings. New version auto-supersedes the prior pending version. Full history always available. Client sees exactly which version is current - no more "which render are we looking at?" confusion.

Per-room organisation

Create rooms within each project: Kitchen, Master Suite, Bathrooms, Living Room, and more. Every material and design is filed under its room. Nothing gets lost in a general project folder.
