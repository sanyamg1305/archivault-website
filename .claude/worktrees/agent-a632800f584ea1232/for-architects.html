The real bottleneck

The problems no one talks about at architecture conferences

📞

"Clients call you mid-design to ask what is happening on site"

Every call is a context switch that kills focus. It also signals to the client that they are not in control of their own project. Neither feeling helps your relationship or your work.

💬

"Approvals get lost in WhatsApp - then disputed at execution"

A voice note or screenshot has no legal weight. When a contractor installs the wrong tile, the WhatsApp thread proves nothing. You absorb the cost, the relationship damage, or both.

💸

"Budget conversations happen at billing time, not during the project"

The client had no visibility for 6 months. Now they are looking at a bill. That produces confrontation, not conversation. And confrontation at the end undoes everything the project achieved.

🤝

"Referrals are random, not systematic"

Every satisfied client is a potential referral worth Rs10L to Rs50L. But if the experience was stressful - even if the output was excellent - they hesitate to recommend you to someone they care about.
