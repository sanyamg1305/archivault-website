The real problem

Architecture firms don't lose clients because of bad design.

They lose them because of what happens around the design. Silence between updates. Approvals nobody can find. Budget surprises at handover. Clients who feel like passengers in their own project.

Clients call mid-project asking what's happening
Every call is a signal you're not in control. By the third call in a week, the client has mentally categorised your firm as one that requires constant chasing.
Approvals buried in WhatsApp, disputed at execution
A client approved the tile layout in a voice note. Three weeks later, the wrong tile is installed. Nobody can find the approval. A dispute begins.
Budget conversations happen at billing time
Presenting unexpected costs at invoice time doesn't feel like a business conversation to the client. It feels like an ambush. And ambushed clients don't refer.
Referrals are random, not systematic
Your best growth channel is being left entirely to chance. Firms that crack the referral code aren't luckier - they deliver a different kind of experience.

How Archivault fixes it

Structured visibility. From day one to handover.

Archivault replaces the chaos of WhatsApp threads and scattered approvals with one structured system - for your team and for your clients.

Client portal with site logs - clients check in, don't call
Every site update, every progress photo, every milestone goes into the client's portal the same day. They stop calling because they're already informed.
Timestamped approval trail on every decision
Every design and material approval is logged with the approving user and exact timestamp. No dispute is possible because the record is unambiguous.
Phase-based budget visibility from day one
Clients see total budget, approved spend, and remaining balance in their portal throughout the project. The invoice is never a surprise because they've been watching it build.
Projects end with excitement, not relief
A client who felt informed and in control throughout refers before the project even ends. That's the referral flywheel - and it starts with the experience you deliver.
