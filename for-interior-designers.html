The interior design problem

More decisions. More disputes. More chaos.

Interior design projects involve more micro-decisions than almost any other professional service. And with that volume of decisions comes a proportional volume of risk - disputed approvals, scope creep, and budget surprises.

"I never approved that tile" - at installation
The client approved it in a WhatsApp voice note. You can't find the message. The wrong tile is already installed. Now everyone is paying for it.
Clients change their mind with no record of what was agreed
Scope creep with no paper trail means extra work you can't charge for. Or worse, work that gets disputed because nobody documented what was originally agreed.
Vendor coordination across 8 different WhatsApp groups
Flooring vendor in one group, furniture in another, electrical in a third. Critical messages get buried. Decisions fall through the cracks. Delays follow.
Budget conversation is dreaded by both sides
When the client has no visibility into costs during the project, the final invoice feels like a surprise - even if every rupee is justified. That feeling kills referrals.

"No more 'final kya decide hua tha?'"

Every material has a status, a cost, an image, and a full approval history. Always.

How it works

From client onboarding to final handover.

Set up a project, invite your client by email, create rooms, add materials and designs. Your client gets a portal link and can approve or revise anything from their phone.

You add materials as you select them
Name, brand, vendor link, cost, and reference image. The material goes into the client's pending approvals the moment you add it.
Client approves or requests revision
One click to approve. Revision request requires a written note explaining what needs to change. No ambiguity about what was asked for.
Budget updates automatically
Approved material costs flow into the budget dashboard. Client sees live spend against total budget, broken down by room, throughout the project.
Export and hand to contractor
When procurement starts, export the full approved material schedule as a print-ready PDF organised by room. The contractor has exactly what they need.
